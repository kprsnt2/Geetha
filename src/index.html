The Bhagavad Gita (భగవద్గీత) is a 700-verse Hindu scripture from the Mahabharata. It is a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna, covering dharma (duty), karma (action), bhakti (devotion), jnana (knowledge), and moksha (liberation).
